## Strategic frame

**RuggedU ($497) is not the prize — it's the proof engine and the top of the consulting funnel.** The money is in RuggedConsulting / RuggedTeams ($2,500–$4,500). The workshop's job is to generate testimonials, case studies, and warm consulting leads.

Because the offer has **no proof yet, trust beats volume.** Lead with warm and borrowed-trust channels; add volume once testimonials exist.

**Two-cohort launch arc:**
- **Cohort 1 — June 23 / 30 / July 7** (Tue 10:30am–noon CT). KPI is **proof, not revenue.** Get 3–6 people you already know into the room, document everything, walk out with 2–3 testimonials + 1 case study.
- **Cohort 2 — August** (clean Tuesdays after travel; tentative Aug 4 / 11 / 18). The first **real fill**, using June's proof.
- Travel blackout weeks: **July 12 and July 26.**

## Channel tier list

_Ranked by leverage for a no-proof-yet launch (whose trust you're borrowing). Email, InMail, phone, and text are **delivery methods** that run across tiers._

### Tier 1 — Borrowed trust & warm (fills cohorts now)
1. **Referral asks to your network** — MIV/Josh/Zack, Augurian + Cloudburst past clients.
2. **Partnerships with people who already sell to trades** — marketing agencies, ServiceTitan/Jobber consultants, bookkeepers/fractional CFOs, trades coaches. Start with the fractional CMO you talked to.
3. **LinkedIn Sales Nav outbound (InMail)** — your strength; target by trade + owner/GM title + metro. Outbound, not feed.
4. **Warm email + phone** to existing contacts.
5. **Partner with AppliedAI** ([appliedai.mn](https://www.appliedai.mn/)) — warm relationship, MN AI nonprofit. **Credibility + content/authority play, NOT a direct trades-lead channel** (their audience is AI-curious professionals, not trades owners). Gets you: meetup talks, podcast guest spot, newsletter/YouTube features, conference stage, nonprofit "in partnership with" halo. Builds authority that makes every other channel convert better.

### Tier 2 — High-trust, set up over a few weeks
1. **★ Co-hosted AI education sessions at a local anchor business** — lumber yards, remodeling/GC firms, realtor/property-mgmt offices, paint stores, equipment rental. You bring the "AI 101," they bring room + audience + credibility. Repeatable roadshow. (Strongest in this tier.)
2. **Host your own "AI for the Trades" event at your office** — self-hosted seminar version; you own the room, date, and recording (content asset). Relies on your Tier 1 outreach to fill the room. **Highest-leverage variant: co-host it with AppliedAI** (their credibility + promotion, your venue + trades content).
3. **Lunch-and-learns / speaking at trade associations** — ACCA (HVAC), PHCC (plumbing), IEC/NECA (electrical) + local chapters.
4. **Supply houses / distributors** — Ferguson, local HVAC/electrical supply; owners are there daily.
5. **Trades podcasts as a guest** — targeted, high-trust, keeps the asset.
6. **BNI & Chamber of Commerce** — contractors live in BNI.

### Tier 3 — Volume & compounding (turn on once cohort 1 gives proof)
1. **Systematized local-provider outreach** — pull shops in 1–2 metros from Google Maps/Yelp → multi-touch.
2. **Phone + text** — underrated; trades owners answer their phones.
3. **Facebook groups & forums** (HVAC-Talk, ContractorTalk, owner groups) — helpful, never spammy.
4. **YouTube / short video** — "what an HVAC owner built in 20 min."
5. **Free "AI 101 for trades" webinar** → cohort funnel.
6. **Direct mail** — stands out because this audience rarely gets it.
7. **Cold email** (list-based).

**Focus:** Tier 1 (referrals + partnerships + Sales Nav) to fill June/August, plus book one Tier 2 co-hosted/office session. 3–4 channels, deep — not all of them.

## KPIs to watch weekly
Outreach sent → reply rate → calls booked → show rate → **close rate** → seats sold → **consulting leads**.

- Healthy replies, low closes = offer/conversation problem.
