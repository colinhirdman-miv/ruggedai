## Pricing: should the workshop be $97?

**Recommendation: keep $497. Do not drop to $97.** It solves the wrong problem.

- **$97 optimizes for signups; Cohort 1 needs committed attendees who produce proof.** The whole job of the first cohort is testimonials + a case study, which requires people who show up all three weeks and do the work. Cheap/low-priced workshops have notoriously bad show-and-finish rates. You'd fill the room and watch half of it evaporate by week 2 — and walk away with weaker proof, the one thing you can't afford.
- **Price signals value.** To a trades owner (who isn't broke and spends real money on tools/software), $97 reads as "cheap, probably fluff." $497 reads as "real, but accessible." Underpricing a B2B offer usually *lowers* perceived quality and attendance intent.
- **It cheapens the funnel.** RuggedU is the top of a ladder to $2,500–$4,500 consulting. If the workshop is $97, the jump to $4,500 is jarring and the value anchor sits on the floor. $497 keeps the ladder coherent.
- **The barrier isn't price — it's trust and time.** $97 fixes neither. The first-session full refund already kills the risk objection without torching the price.

**If filling Cohort 1 fast is the worry:** use a one-time, private **$297 founding price** in 1:1 outreach ("first group, here's a price"), then back to $497. Keeps commitment and positioning intact. Far better than a fire-sale to $97. Keep this off the public site (no founding-cohort framing on the page).
