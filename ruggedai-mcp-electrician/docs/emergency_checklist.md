# Emergency Caller Checklist

Instructions to give callers while they wait for a technician.

## If There's Smoke or Burning Smell
1. Do NOT touch the panel, outlets, or any switches
2. If smoke is visible, evacuate and call 911 immediately
3. If no visible smoke, locate your main breaker and turn off power to the affected area only if you can do so safely
4. Do not use the affected circuits until our tech arrives
5. Keep the area clear and ventilated

## If Power Is Out (Partial or Full)
1. Check if neighbors have power — if not, call your utility company first (may be their issue)
2. Check your main breaker panel — look for any tripped breakers (middle position)
3. Do NOT repeatedly reset a tripped breaker — if it trips again immediately, stop and wait for us
4. Unplug sensitive electronics (TVs, computers) to protect from surge when power returns
5. If you have medical equipment on electricity, let us know immediately

## If an Outlet Is Sparking
1. Do not use the outlet
2. Unplug anything connected to it
3. If sparking continues without anything plugged in, turn off the breaker for that circuit
4. Do not attempt to open or inspect the outlet yourself

## For Commercial Customers
1. Notify your facilities manager or building owner immediately
2. Document the issue with photos if safe to do so
3. Do not allow employees to work near the affected area
4. If the issue affects your ability to operate, let us know — we prioritize commercial emergencies

## Information to Have Ready When Tech Arrives
- Location of main electrical panel
- Any recent work done on electrical system
- When the issue started
- What was happening right before the issue (storm, appliance use, etc.)
- Any other symptoms (flickering lights, burning smell elsewhere, etc.)
